"okay but for real what is ekphrasis!!!"

that's a really good question! sounds like a sneeze! what a consonant cluster!
i am always tapping this word, or wearing it like a little flight attendant wing on my shirt,
so i think it is important, here, to clarify that, while the term
has semi-specific origins (e.g. poetry about art, or poetry in close description),
for me, the ekphrastic mode is a dispositional orientation which has taken on something of a vocational weight.


this clarity of ekphrasis erupted by accident, or it took, like any concentric buildup, a very long time.
the first things i ever wrote were ekphrasis: to tamper the propulsive frenzy that came when i consumed certain media,
i wrote behind my own back about music (for lotusland, bearded, exclaim!, etc.),
then books (for adroit, prism, the montreal review of books, etc.), served as co-reviews editor for the ex-puritan for three years, was a columnist, columnist, columnist.

all i had been was 20, newly disabled, alone for many months. i didn't know what it was called --
but the clarity was that this writing helped me touch something other than just the end of myself.
structurally, inherently, ekphrasis let me touch down in the world, or at least stretch, reach.


as i entered the world, slowly, attending my first poetry readings,
i felt strange, speaking aloud for the first time in weeks, sensing only really in glimpses anyone thinking deeply
about how strange/special it was to be gathered for sound. what it was to collect.


so, with new friends, i tried other things: with nadia davoli, playd8s, an interview series about play;
with many friends, catch, an audio poetry/meditation podcast about how things feel;
with jack mosher, oranging, a gesturally-based adaptation of renee gladman’s novel, event factory, for the stage.


when i moved to the place where the trees stand in the water,
i got the chance, for the first time, to host: manahil bandukwala and conyer clayton’s co-book launch
at glad day books. writing ekphrasis to their books, their friendship, something clicked.


it became clear to me what ekphrasis was for -- not the writing-about, but the -through. -to.
to a friend, or the hope of one.


since then, i have hosted book launches, workshops, artists talks,
memory island with lily wang, cason sharpe, and anna julia stainsby,
to all the birds we hear in trees but never see with sanna wani and elizabeth mudenyo. i wrote a book about ekphrasis, but that part is a secret.
soon, i will do other things.
mostly, i will hope for the chance to be a good friend.


basically, for me, ekphrasis is *the* gathering point - the cluster of language, medium, language, someone, someone, us.
it is what i am, what i do, where i am going, where i want to be (together, truly).
